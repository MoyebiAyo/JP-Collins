1. Acceptance of Terms

By accessing this website, registering for a programme, attending a workshop, or volunteering with JPCollins Financial Literacy Organisation ("JPCollins", "we", "us"), you agree to be bound by these Terms & Conditions. If you do not agree, please do not use our website or participate in our programmes.

For participants under 18, a parent or legal guardian must read and accept these terms on the minor's behalf, and the host school must grant formal written approval before programme commencement.

2. Who May Participate

Our programmes are designed for three audiences:

  • Teenagers aged 14–19 — with school and/or parental consent.
  • Young professionals aged 20–35.
  • Women entrepreneurs of any adult age.

Programmes for teenagers are delivered exclusively within or under the formal authority of a host school or community institution.

3. Programme Structure & Delivery

Our core Financial Literacy Programme runs for six (6) weeks and covers budgeting, credit awareness, saving, investment literacy, entrepreneurship, and climate credit awareness. Programme format, duration, and content may be adapted to suit the host institution. We reserve the right to modify, reschedule, or cancel a programme with reasonable notice.

4. Code of Conduct

All participants, volunteers, staff, and partners are expected to act with honesty, respect, and integrity. The following are strictly prohibited:

  • Any form of harassment, discrimination, bullying, or intimidation.
  • Disruptive behaviour that affects the learning of others.
  • Sharing false, misleading, or harmful financial advice using the JPCollins name.
  • Any conduct that violates our Safeguarding Policy.

JPCollins reserves the right to remove any participant or volunteer who breaches this code.

5. Safeguarding Commitment

Zero-tolerance policy. JPCollins maintains a zero-tolerance policy for any form of emotional, verbal, physical, or sexual abuse, harassment, or exploitation. All volunteers and facilitators must undergo a basic orientation on our safeguarding standards and formally acknowledge our Safeguarding Policy before engaging with students.

6. Certificates

Participants who successfully complete a programme and meet its requirements are awarded a Certificate of Financial Literacy. Certificates are issued at JPCollins's discretion and may be withheld if requirements are not met or if the code of conduct is breached. Certificates are non-transferable.

You may not copy, reproduce, distribute, or adapt our materials without prior written permission. Participants retain ownership of their own personal work and reflections.

8. Media & Testimonial Consent

From time to time we may wish to photograph or record programme activities for impact reporting and awareness. No photographs, video, or audio of any participant — especially minors — will be captured or used without explicit, documented consent from the individual and, for minors, from the school administration and/or parent or guardian. You may withdraw media consent at any time by contacting us.
