1. Overview

JPCollins Financial Literacy Organisation ("JPCollins", "we", "us") is committed to protecting the privacy of every teenager, young professional, woman entrepreneur, parent, educator, volunteer, and partner who interacts with our programmes and website. This policy explains what personal information we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, and the rights you have over it.

Our work with teenagers aged 14–19 means we treat all data — and especially data relating to minors — with the highest standard of care, in line with the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023, the EU/UK GDPR, and the principles of the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child.

2. Information We Collect

Information you provide directly

  • Contact details — name, email address, phone number, and location submitted through our contact or registration forms.
  • Programme enrolment data — age, school or institution, and programme preferences when you register for a workshop.
  • Messages — the content of enquiries, feedback, or testimonials you choose to share with us.
  • Newsletter subscriptions — your email address if you opt in to updates.

Information collected automatically

  • Usage data — pages visited, referring links, and approximate location, collected via privacy-friendly analytics.
  • Cookies — small files that remember your preferences (see our Cookie Policy).

3. How We Use Your Information

We only process personal information for clearly defined, legitimate purposes:

  • To deliver, administer, and improve our financial literacy programmes.
  • To respond to your enquiries and provide requested information.
  • To process programme registrations and issue certificates of completion.
  • To communicate programme updates, resources, and opportunities (only with your consent).
  • To capture consented media (photos/video) for impact reporting and awareness — strictly with documented consent.
  • To meet legal, safeguarding, and accountability obligations.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal data under one or more of the following lawful bases:

  • Consent — you have freely agreed (e.g. newsletter, media consent).
  • Contract — necessary to deliver a programme you have enrolled in.
  • Legal obligation — to comply with safeguarding and data protection law.
  • Legitimate interest — to operate and improve our organisation, balanced against your privacy rights.

5. Sharing Your Information

We do not sell personal data. We only share information when necessary:

  • With host schools and partner institutions — to coordinate programme delivery (only what is operationally necessary).
  • With trusted service providers — such as email or hosting platforms, under written data-processing agreements.
  • With authorities — where required by law, or to protect the safety of a participant under our safeguarding obligations.

Student & minor data is treated with heightened protection. We never share a participant's personal data for marketing, and we never share it without the knowledge of the host school and, where required, the parent or guardian.

6. Student & Minor Data (Safeguarding)

Because our programmes serve teenagers aged 14–19, the following rules apply to all data relating to minors:

  • We obtain parental or school consent before collecting, processing, or publishing any data relating to a minor.
  • Personal information collected in connection with programme delivery, attendance, incident reporting, or consent is limited to what is necessary, stored securely, and shared only with authorised persons.
  • All media (photos, video, audio) of participants requires explicit, documented consent from the school administration and/or parents/legal guardians before capture or use.
  • Any safeguarding-related data is handled in line with applicable child protection laws and our Safeguarding Policy.

7. Data Retention

We keep personal information only for as long as necessary for the purpose it was collected:

  • Programme records — retained for the duration of the programme plus a reasonable period for certification and audit.
  • Safeguarding records — retained securely per legal and child protection requirements.
  • Marketing data — retained until you withdraw consent or unsubscribe.

When data is no longer needed, it is securely deleted or anonymised.

8. Data Security

We apply appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including secure storage, access limited to authorised personnel, and encryption in transit. Access to data relating to minors is further restricted to designated safeguarding and programme staff only.

9. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Request deletion of your personal data (subject to legal exceptions).
  • Withdraw consent at any time for consent-based processing.
  • Object to or restrict certain types of processing.
  • Lodge a complaint with a data protection authority.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details below.

10. Cookies

Our website uses a minimal set of cookies to function properly and understand aggregate usage. We do not use cookies for cross-site advertising tracking. For full details, see our Cookie Policy.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. The "Effective Date" at the top of this page indicates the latest revision. Material changes will be highlighted on our website.
