1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They allow the site to remember your actions and preferences over time, so you don't have to re-enter information each time you return. Cookies are widely used across the web and are not inherently harmful — they do not contain viruses and cannot access other files on your device.

2. How We Use Cookies

JPCollins uses cookies sparingly and purposefully. We use them to:

  • Keep our website functioning correctly.
  • Remember your preferences (such as menu state or scrolled sections).
  • Understand, in aggregate, how visitors use our site so we can improve it.
  • Embed third-party content such as YouTube videos.

We do not use cookies to sell your data, build advertising profiles, or track you across unrelated websites. Our use of cookies is limited to operating and improving a nonprofit educational website.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Strictly Necessary Cookies

These are essential for the website to function. They enable core features like navigation and security. The website cannot work properly without them. These do not require consent.

Functional Cookies

These remember choices you make (such as whether you've opened the mobile menu) to provide a smoother experience. They do not collect identifying information.

Analytics Cookies

We may use privacy-friendly analytics to understand aggregate traffic patterns — for example, which pages are most visited. This data is anonymised and used only to improve our content.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Our website embeds content from third parties — most notably YouTube videos. When you click play on an embedded video, YouTube may set cookies on your device subject to YouTube's own privacy policy. We recommend reviewing Google's Privacy Policy for details.

We also embed no external advertising networks that set tracking cookies.

5. Managing and Deleting Cookies

You are always in control. Most web browsers let you:

  • View the cookies stored on your device.
  • Block all or specific cookies.
  • Delete existing cookies.
  • Set preferences to clear cookies when you close your browser.

Controls differ by browser. Here are official guides for the most common ones:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable all cookies, our website will still work — you will still be able to read content and navigate. However, some convenience features (such as remembering menu state or embedded video playback) may be limited.

7. Changes to This Policy

If we change how we use cookies, we will update this page and revise the "Effective Date" at the top.
