2. Legal Status & Operations

JPCollins operates its Teens Financial Literacy Programme under the explicit legal and operational framework of host schools and community institutions. Formal, written approval from the hosting educational institution or community authority must be obtained before any programme commencement. This approval constitutes the primary local permission required to conduct our programmes.

3. Global Safeguarding Policy Statement

At JPCollins, we are committed to empowering youth with financial literacy in an environment that is safe, respectful, and secure. As we expand globally, we align our safeguarding practices with international child protection standards, including the principles of the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child.

This document outlines the non-negotiable safeguarding principles that govern all JPCollins programmes — protecting both the teenagers we serve and the integrity of our international brand and partners.

4. Local Permission & School Approval

Formal Authorization

Before any programme commencement, formal, written approval must be obtained from the hosting educational institution or community authority.

Jurisdictional Framework

JPCollins operates under the explicit legal and operational framework of the host school. The formal approval by school leadership constitutes the primary local permission required to conduct the programme.

5. On-Site School Supervision

The "Never Alone" Rule

JPCollins staff, volunteers, and advisors are facilitators of knowledge, not primary caretakers.

Mandatory Presence

A designated school authority, teacher, or local coordinator must be physically present in the room or immediate vicinity at all times during programme delivery. No JPCollins personnel may be left entirely alone with students.

6. Volunteer & Facilitator Conduct

Professional Boundaries

All representatives of JPCollins must maintain strictly professional, age-appropriate, and culturally respectful behaviour.

Zero Tolerance

JPCollins maintains a zero-tolerance policy for any form of emotional, verbal, physical, or sexual abuse, harassment, or exploitation. All volunteers must undergo a basic orientation on these standards prior to engaging with students.

7. Communication with Students

No Private Contact

Representatives are strictly prohibited from engaging in private, one-on-one communication with participating teenagers. This includes sharing personal phone numbers, email addresses, or connecting via personal social media accounts.

Official Channels

Any necessary out-of-session communication regarding the curriculum or programme updates must be routed exclusively through official school channels or approved JPCollins platforms with parental/guardian visibility.

8. Media, Photo, & Video Consent

Prior Consent

No photographs, video recordings, or audio interviews of participants may be captured or utilised for global branding, reports, or social media without explicit, documented consent from the school administration and/or parents/legal guardians.

Dignity in Representation

All marketing and media materials must depict the teenagers with dignity, focusing on their educational achievements and empowerment.

9. Incident Reporting, Data Privacy & Accountability

Immediate Escalation

Any suspected breach of this policy, disclosure of harm by a student, or behavioural concern must be reported within 24 hours to the JPCollins Project Executive and the host school's safeguarding lead.

Named Safeguarding Contact

The designated safeguarding contact for the programme is Blessing Nnakife — Human Resources / Admin, reachable via jpcollins2@yahoo.com or +234 701 385 0685. All safeguarding concerns should be directed through this official contact and the host school's safeguarding lead.

Data Privacy

Any personal information collected in connection with programme delivery, incident reporting, attendance, or consent must be limited to what is necessary, stored securely, shared only with authorised persons, and handled in line with applicable data protection laws and school requirements. See our full Privacy Policy.

Whistleblower Protection

JPCollins ensures a safe, confidential environment for reporting. We will cooperate fully with local authorities and school governing bodies to investigate any incidents transparently and swiftly.

10. Compliance Notice

Adherence to this Safeguarding Policy is a mandatory condition of partnership, employment, and volunteer engagement with the JPCollins Teens Financial Literacy Programme worldwide.

All volunteers and facilitators must read and formally acknowledge this policy before participating in any programme activity. Non-compliance will result in the immediate termination of the engagement and, where applicable, legal referral.
